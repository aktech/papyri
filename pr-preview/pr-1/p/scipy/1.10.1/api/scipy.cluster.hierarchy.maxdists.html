Given a linkage matrix ``Z``, `scipy.cluster.hierarchy.maxdists` computes for each new cluster generated (i.e., for each row of the linkage matrix) what is the maximum distance between any two child clusters. Due to the nature of hierarchical clustering, in many cases this is going to be just the distance between the two child clusters that were merged to form the current one - that is, Z[:,2]. However, for non-monotonic cluster assignments such as `scipy.cluster.hierarchy.median` clustering this is not always the case: There may be cluster formations were the distance between the two clusters merged is smaller than the distance between their children.
